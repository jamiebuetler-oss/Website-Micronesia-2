About the Mission

A Pacific voice in the heart of multilateral Geneva.

Opened on 22 April 2024, the Mission is the FSM's first diplomatic presence in Europe, representing the country across the United Nations system, the World Health Organization, the European Union and Switzerland.

As a low-lying Pacific Island nation, the FSM brings a distinct perspective to Geneva: ocean governance, climate resilience and the future of small island states.

Priorities

Where the Mission concentrates its work.

Ocean & Climate

Advancing climate finance, the high seas, and conservation of marine biodiversity beyond national jurisdiction.

Human Rights

Participating in the Universal Periodic Review and treaty body processes shaping the FSM's national reporting.

Global Health

Carrying the Asia-Pacific voice on the WHO Executive Council, with a focus on resilience and NCDs.

Sustainable Development

Tracking progress on the SDGs and the development needs of small island developing states.

AS
Permanent Representative

H.E. Akillino H. Susaia

Sworn in in Palikir, Pohnpei, before taking up his post in Geneva, Ambassador Susaia brings more than two decades of public service — including roles as FSM Consul General in Hawai i and FSM Ambassador to China and the United States.

Under his leadership the Mission has focused on the UN human rights system, the WHO Executive Council, and ocean-governance negotiations such as the BBNJ Agreement.
